FAQ

Questions? We've Got Answers.

What is Q-BRAM and what makes it different?
+
Q-BRAM (Quantitative Breadth Regime Analysis Model) is a proprietary 5-regime market classification system developed on 19 years of NIFTY 500 data. It combines 50+ breadth metrics into a single composite score. The key insight: signals taken in Expansion regime show a 61.8% win rate vs 34% in Distribution. Most traders use only price. Q-BRAM uses the internal health of the entire market.
Is this only for Indian markets?
+
The platform is India-first — full NSE/BSE coverage, NIFTY 500 universe, SEBI PIT insider filings, and FII/DII institutional data. US market (Russell 3000) is partially integrated and expanding. Indian systematic traders get maximum value from the platform today.
How is this different from Tickertape, Screener.in, or Chartink?
+
Those tools are stock-level screeners. Quantum Breadth 360 is a market-breadth intelligence terminal — it tells you the health of the overall market and where institutional money is flowing before you look at individual stocks. The Trading Journal also ties every trade to its regime — something no other platform in India offers.
What happens after the 14-day Pro trial?
+
After your trial, you're automatically moved to the Explorer free tier — no charges, no surprises. Your data and trade history are preserved. You can upgrade to Pro anytime from inside the app to restore full access.
Can I cancel my Pro subscription?
+
Yes, anytime from the Billing section in your account. Cancellation takes effect at the end of your current billing period — you keep full Pro access until then. No lock-in, no questions asked.
Is this suitable for intraday traders?
+
Quantum Breadth 360 is optimised for swing and positional traders with 2-day to 8-week holding periods — using Minervini SEPA, O'Neil CAN SLIM, Qullamaggie momentum, and Weinstein Stage Analysis. Intraday traders will find limited utility as the breadth data is most useful over multi-day timeframes.
