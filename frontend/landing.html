QUESTIONS
Frequently Asked
What is Q-BRAM and what makes it different?
+
Q-BRAM (Quantitative Breadth Regime Analysis Model) is a proprietary 5-regime market classification framework developed and backtested on 19 years of NIFTY 500 data. It combines 50+ breadth metrics — A/D line, DMA crossings, new highs/lows, thrust indicators — into a single composite score. The key insight: signals taken in the Expansion regime show a 61.8% win rate vs 34% in Distribution. Most traders use price alone. Q-BRAM uses the internal health of the entire market.
Is this only for Indian markets?
+
The platform is India-first — full NSE/BSE coverage, NIFTY 500 universe, SEBI PIT insider filings, FII/DII institutional data, and NSE sector taxonomy. US market (Russell 3000) data is partially integrated and expanding. Indian systematic traders will get the maximum value from the platform today.
How is this different from Tickertape, Screener.in, or Chartink?
+
Those tools are stock-level screeners and fundamental databases. Quantum Breadth 360 is a market-breadth intelligence terminal — it tells you the health of the overall market, where institutional money is flowing, and which sectors are leading before you even look at individual stocks. The Trading Journal then ties every trade back to its market regime — something no other platform in India does.

Is this suitable for intraday traders?
+
Quantum Breadth 360 is optimised for swing and positional traders (2-day to 8-week holding periods) using methodologies like Minervini SEPA, O'Neil CAN SLIM, Qullamaggie momentum, and Weinstein Stage Analysis. The breadth and regime data is most valuable for traders whose holding periods are long enough for market context to matter. Intraday traders will find limited utility.
